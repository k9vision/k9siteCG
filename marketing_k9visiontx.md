# K9 Vision — Posting Your Videos to Instagram, TikTok & Facebook (from your iPhone)

A simple, no-jargon guide to getting your dog-training videos onto all three
platforms with the least effort and **$0 in tools**.

---

## The goal
Record a clip on your iPhone → post it to **Instagram, TikTok, and Facebook**
quickly, for free, without hiring anyone or learning complicated software.

There's a tiny bit of one-time setup, then it becomes a 5-minute routine.

---

## Step 1 — One-time setup (do this once; the part most people skip)

These two things are required before *any* app can post to Instagram and Facebook
for you. It's free and takes about 10 minutes total.

1. **Switch Instagram to a Creator (or Business) account**
   - In Instagram: **Settings → Account type and tools → Switch to professional
     account → Creator.**
   - It's free, you keep all your followers, and it unlocks posting tools and
     basic stats (how many people saw each video).

2. **Create a Facebook *Page* (not your personal profile) and link it to
   Instagram**
   - A "Page" is the business version of Facebook (e.g., "K9 Vision Dog
     Training"). Personal profiles can't be auto-posted to — only Pages can.
   - When setting up, **link the Page to your Instagram Creator account** (Meta
     will prompt you, or do it in the Page's settings).

> **Why this matters:** Instagram and Facebook (both owned by "Meta") only allow
> apps to post to *professional* accounts and *Pages*. Without this step, nothing
> below will work. TikTok needs nothing special — your normal account is fine.

---

## Step 2 — Pick how you'll post (two easy paths)

### ⭐ Path A — Simplest & most reliable (recommended to start)
Use the apps the platforms make themselves. Two apps, both free:

- **Instagram + Facebook together:** Install the free **Meta Business Suite** app.
  It posts and schedules to your Instagram and Facebook Page at the same time.
  - *Even simpler:* when you post a Reel in Instagram, just turn ON the
    **"Also share to Facebook"** toggle. One post → both platforms.
- **TikTok:** Post directly in the **TikTok app**. (TikTok works best when you
  post inside its own app, and it's the one platform other tools don't handle as
  smoothly.)

**Pros:** rock-solid, totally free, nothing extra to learn.
**Con:** you touch two apps (the Instagram/Meta side, and TikTok).

### Path B — One app for all three at once
If switching between two apps feels annoying, use a free scheduler that pushes a
single video to **all three** platforms:

- **Buffer — free plan.** Connect Instagram, TikTok, and Facebook (3 channels =
  exactly your three). Upload one video, write one caption, post to all three.
  Has an iPhone app.
- **Alternative: Metricool — free plan.** Same idea, with a bit more stats.

**Pros:** one upload → everywhere; can schedule posts ahead of time.
**Con:** free plans have limits (a capped number of channels and scheduled posts).
Fine for a small business, just don't expect unlimited.

> **Quick honesty note:** You may have heard "use free open-source software."
> Those exist (Postiz, Mixpost), but they require running your own server and
> technical upkeep — not something to log into from a phone. For "just open an app
> and post," the free apps above are the right call.

## Step 3 — Your repeatable posting routine

1. **Record** the clip on your iPhone (good light, hold the phone **vertically**).
2. **Trim & polish (optional, free):** open it in **CapCut** (free iPhone app) to
   cut dead space, add text, and **captions/subtitles**.
3. **Post:**
   - *Path A:* Post the Reel in Instagram with **"Also share to Facebook"** on →
     then post the same clip in the **TikTok** app.
   - *Path B:* Open **Buffer**, upload once, write the caption, pick Instagram +
     TikTok + Facebook, hit post (or schedule for later).
4. **Done.** That's all three platforms covered.

## 🎬 The .MOV fix — make your iPhone videos look right (not washed-out)

Your iPhone saves videos as **.MOV files**, and by default it uses a space-saving
format called **HEVC** with **HDR** color. Instagram, TikTok, and Facebook all
*accept* .MOV — but they **re-compress every video** you upload, and HDR/HEVC
footage often comes out **washed-out, too dark, or oddly tinted** on other people's
phones. It's the #1 reason a clip that looked great on your screen looks "off" once
it's posted.

Good news: it's a **one-time setting**, and clips you've already shot are easy to fix.

### ✅ Best fix — change one setting, and every future video is ready
Do this once on your iPhone:
1. **Settings → Camera → Formats → tap "Most Compatible."**
   This records in the standard **H.264** format every app handles perfectly — still full HD.
2. **Settings → Camera → Record Video → turn OFF "HDR Video."**
   Now your colors look the same everywhere instead of shifting after upload.

That's it — from now on your videos are already in the safe format, and you lose
nothing that matters for social media.

### 🔧 Already shot a bunch of clips? Convert them the easy way
For videos you filmed *before* changing that setting:
- **Easiest:** open the clip in **CapCut** (the free app from Step 3), trim/caption it,
  and hit **Export.** CapCut always saves a clean, standard **MP4** — so just editing
  and posting *from CapCut* fixes the problem automatically. No extra step.
- **On a computer?** Use **CapCut for desktop** (also free) to open the .MOV and export an MP4.

### 📐 The "right" video, in one line
Aim for **vertical 9:16, 1080×1920, MP4 (H.264), ~30 fps, 15–45 seconds.** That single
format is ideal for all three platforms:

| Platform | Accepts | Best length | Upload limit |
|---|---|---|---|
| **TikTok** | MP4 / .MOV (H.264) | ~21–34 sec | ~288 MB in the app; up to 4 GB from tiktok.com |
| **Instagram Reels** | MP4 / .MOV (H.264) | 15–30 sec | 1 GB |
| **Facebook Reels** | MP4 (H.264) | 15–60 sec (90 sec max) | large (up to 4 GB) |

> **You don't need to memorize this.** If your clip is a short, vertical **MP4 from
> CapCut**, it's already inside every limit above.

### ⚠️ One thing NOT to do
Don't **download your posted TikTok** (the one with the little TikTok watermark) and
re-upload it to Instagram or Facebook. All three platforms **quietly bury** videos that
carry a competitor's watermark or look "recycled." Instead: **export the clean file
once**, then **upload it fresh inside each app.** Same video, posted natively three
times — that's what the algorithms reward.

### 💻 If your .MOV files are on a computer (not your phone)
Two easy options:
- **Post from the computer:** convert with **CapCut desktop**, then upload in a browser —
  **tiktok.com**, **instagram.com**, and **Meta Business Suite** (for your Facebook Page)
  all let you upload video straight from the web.
- **Or send them to your phone:** **AirDrop** (or Google Photos) the clips to your iPhone
  and post in the apps as usual — in-app posting is usually smoothest, especially for TikTok.

Either way, finish with the same call to action: **"Book at k9visiontx.com."**

## Step 4 — Tips that make dog-training videos perform

- **Shoot vertical (9:16)** — full-screen on phones; what Reels/TikTok reward.
- **Hook in the first 2 seconds** — show the result or the cute dog *immediately*
  ("Watch this reactive dog calm down in 30 seconds").
- **Add captions/subtitles** — most people watch with the sound OFF. CapCut can
  auto-generate them.
- **Keep it short** — 15–45 seconds works best for training clips.
- **Use a few relevant hashtags**, e.g.:
  `#dogtraining #houstondogtrainer #k9vision #puppytraining #dogtrainer #houstondogs`
- **Post consistently** — aim for **2–3 times a week**. One good clip can be
  reused across all three platforms.
- **End with a soft call to action** — "Book a session at k9visiontx.com" or
  "DM us to get started."

## Content ideas — never wonder "what do I post?"

### 🎥 10 video ideas (Reels / TikTok)
1. **Before & after** — a dog on day 1 vs. after training (the single best-
   performing format; show the transformation).
2. **30-second tip** — one quick fix, e.g. "Stop leash pulling in 30 seconds."
3. **Common mistake** — "3 things you're doing that make jumping worse," then the
   fix.
4. **A command, broken down** — teach "place," "heel," or "leave it" step by step.
5. **Real session clip** — a short, honest moment from an actual training session
   (people love seeing the real work).
6. **Myth-busting** — "You CAN teach an old dog new tricks — here's proof."
7. **Day in the life** — a fun, fast montage of you working with several dogs.
8. **Reactive/anxious dog win** — a calm-down moment (great emotional hook).
9. **Q&A / "you asked"** — answer a real client question on camera.
10. **Breed or puppy spotlight** — quick tips for a specific breed or puppy stage
    (e.g. "Training a German Shepherd puppy: start here").

### 📸 10 picture ideas (Instagram / Facebook posts)
1. **Happy graduate** — a trained dog sitting proudly (tag the owner if they're OK
   with it).
2. **Before & after photo pair** — side-by-side of behavior or obedience progress.
3. **Action shot** — a dog mid-"sit," "down," or off-leash heel.
4. **You + a client's dog** — builds trust; people hire the *person*.
5. **Cute/funny dog face** — pure engagement bait; caption with a training tip.
6. **Quote graphic** — a short training tip or motivational line on a branded
   background (Canva has free templates).
7. **Client testimonial** — a screenshot of a kind review/text (with permission).
8. **"Trick of the week"** — a photo + caption explaining how to teach it.
9. **Behind the scenes** — your training gear, treats, or setup.
10. **Local pride** — a Houston landmark/park with a dog ("Training in [park] today
    🐾") to reach local clients.

> **Pro move:** turn any photo idea into a video and any video into a photo — one
> training session can become a week of posts across all three platforms.

## Where to promote K9 Vision locally (Houston / Harris / Montgomery / Waller)

Real local pages and channels to get the business in front of dog owners across
your service area. **Read this first:** most pet "lost & found" and rescue pages
**ban self-promotion** — posting an ad there can get you removed. So each channel
below is tagged with *how* to use it: **POST** directly, **PARTNER** (build a
relationship), or **LIST** (get found). Always read a group's rules and lead with
value, never spam.

> ⚠️ Group names and links change over time. Verify each one still exists and
> check its posting rules before you post.

### ✅ POST directly — community / neighborhood / marketplace groups (most promo-friendly)
These usually welcome local small businesses (many have a weekly "Promo Day"):
- **Moms in The Woodlands, Magnolia, Conroe +** — https://www.facebook.com/groups/1584114452111529/
- **The Woodlands, Magnolia & Conroe, TX Market** — https://www.facebook.com/groups/thewoodlandsmagnoliaconroetxmarket/
- **Woodlands Moms** — https://www.facebook.com/groups/wdlsmoms/
- **Sunnyside Community – Waller County (Brookshire)** — https://www.facebook.com/sunnysidetx/
- **Do-it-yourself finds:** on Facebook, search each town for **"[City] Buy/Sell/Trade,"
  "Neighbors of [City],"** and **"[City] Community"** (Cypress, Katy, Hockley,
  Waller, Magnolia, Conroe, Spring, Tomball). Most have thousands of local members.
- **Nextdoor** — claim a free business page; post in your own + neighboring areas.

### 🐕 ENGAGE / post events — dog-owner social groups
Great for goodwill, demos, and event posts (offer a free "training tips" meetup):
- **Houston Active Dogs (Meetup)** — https://www.meetup.com/houston-active-dogs/
- **Zoomies Dog Park & Social Club (Houston)** — https://zoomiesparks.com/
- **Breed groups** — search Meetup/Facebook for "Houston Frenchie," "Houston Italian
  Greyhound," "Houston [breed]" clubs; breed owners often want specialized training.
- *Note:* Canine Country Club (Katy) is a peer/competitor — a networking contact,
  not a place to advertise.

### 🤝 PARTNER — shelters & rescues (don't advertise; build a referral pipeline)
The biggest win for a trainer. Offer a **free/discounted first session for adopters**,
do a **demo at an adoption event**, or become their **go-to referred trainer**:
- **BARC – City of Houston** — https://www.houstontx.gov/barc/
- **Harris County Pets** — https://www.facebook.com/HarrisCountyPets/
- **Houston Humane Society** — https://www.houstonhumane.org/
- **Montgomery County Animal Shelter (MCAS)** — https://www.facebook.com/MCASTX/
- **Humane Society of Montgomery County** — https://www.facebook.com/HumaneSocietyMontgomery/
- **Friends of MCTAS** — https://www.fmctas.org/
- **Safe Haven Animal Rescue (Cypress)**, **Katy's Dog Place Rescue**, **Special Pals** — https://specialpals.org/
- **Also partner with:** local **vets, groomers, pet stores, and doggy daycares** —
  ask to leave cards / cross-refer. Warmest leads you'll find.

### 📣 Lost & found pet groups (huge reach — but usually NO ads; use carefully)
Don't drop an ad here. Instead build name recognition by **helping** (share a free
"what to do if your dog gets loose" post the admins approve). Big ones:
- Houston / Harris County "Lost and Found Pets" groups (several) + PawBoost pages
- **Katy, Texas Lost & Found Pets** (24k+ members) — https://www.facebook.com/KatyTexasLostAndFoundPets/
- **Cypress, TX – Lost Pets (PawBoost)** — https://www.facebook.com/LostPetsCypressTX/
- **Harris County, TX Lost and Found Pets** — https://www.facebook.com/groups/HarrisCountyLostandfound/

### 🔎 LIST — get found (non-Facebook; highest return for new clients)
- **Google Business Profile** — *do this first.* Free, and it's how people find a
  "dog trainer near me" on Google + Maps. Add photos, hours, service area, link to
  k9visiontx.com, and ask happy clients for reviews.
- **Nextdoor** business page (recommendations spread fast here).
- **Yelp** — you already have reviews; keep them fresh.
- **Local directories:** Woodlands Online (Pets section), Community Impact (Houston metro).
- If you ever add boarding/walking: **Rover** and **Wag**.

> **Bottom line:** A solid **Google Business Profile** + **2–3 shelter/vet
> partnerships** will bring in more clients than blasting every Facebook group —
> and won't get you banned. Use the groups to stay visible, not to spam.
